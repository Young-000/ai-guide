**Summary**: Claude Fable 5 and Mythos 5 returned to global users on July 1 after the US Commerce Department lifted the export restriction it imposed on June 12, following a jailbreak discovery by Amazon researchers.

### Key Facts
- June 12: US export controls imposed after Amazon found a jailbreak in Fable 5 — all global access suspended within hours
- June 30: Controls lifted after Anthropic agreed to: deploy the jailbreak patch, collaborate with the US government on future powerful-model standards, and establish proactive security disclosure protocols
- July 1: Service resumed on Claude.ai, Claude Platform, Claude Code, and Claude Cowork; Mythos 5 re-enabled for authorized US organizations
- July 7: Fable 5 transitions to usage-credits billing model

### Why It Matters
This was the first documented case of a single jailbreak vulnerability forcing a ~19-day global shutdown of a frontier AI model. Enterprises that treated Fable 5 as essential infrastructure faced an unexpected, government-driven outage with hours of notice. Resilience planning for AI-dependent workflows must now account for sovereign AI risk — not just provider downtime.
