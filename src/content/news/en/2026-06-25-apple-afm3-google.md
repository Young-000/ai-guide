**Summary**: Apple at WWDC 2026 unveiled its third-generation Apple Foundation Models (AFM 3), a five-model family co-developed with Google and NVIDIA that pushes on-device intelligence to 20 billion parameters while extending Private Cloud Compute to Google Cloud infrastructure.

### Key Facts
- **AFM 3 Core** (3B dense): next-gen on-device model with improved quality across Apple Intelligence tasks
- **AFM 3 Core Advanced** (20B sparse multimodal): Apple's most powerful on-device model to date — sparse architecture activates only **1–4B parameters per request**, keeping it viable on Apple silicon
- **AFM 3 Cloud / ADM 3 Cloud / AFM 3 Cloud Pro**: server-side models on Private Cloud Compute, with Cloud Pro running on **NVIDIA GPUs in Google Cloud** under the same privacy guarantees
- The release is the first tangible output of the **Apple–Google multi-year partnership** announced in January 2026

### Why It Matters
Apple has long staked its AI story on on-device privacy. Openly leveraging Google Cloud for its highest-tier model is a meaningful shift. The 20B sparse on-device model raises the bar for what's expected from smartphone-class AI, and sets up a direct benchmark against competing on-device approaches from Google and Samsung.
