**Summary**: OpenAI has moved beyond API sales, establishing a dedicated deployment subsidiary with 19 global consulting and investment partners to drive AI into the operational core of large enterprises.

### Key Facts
- 19 partners committed $4B+: TPG (lead), Bain, McKinsey, Capgemini, Goldman Sachs, SoftBank, Brookfield, Advent International
- OpenAI holds a controlling majority stake; consulting partners co-execute AI transformation engagements with their existing clients
- June 14 follow-up: OpenAI Partner Network launched with $150M committed — Accenture, BCG, PwC among founding members
- Focus areas: supply chain, HR, finance — integrating GPT-5-class models into mission-critical workflows

### Why It Matters
This signals a strategic pivot from selling model access to owning enterprise AI delivery. By co-investing with the world's top consultancies, OpenAI gains a direct channel into board-level transformation budgets — a move that threatens traditional IT services firms and raises the stakes for competitors like Google and Anthropic.
