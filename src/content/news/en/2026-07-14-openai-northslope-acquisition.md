**One-line summary**: OpenAI's enterprise deployment arm has acquired Northslope, whose forward-deployed engineers live inside customer organizations across healthcare, energy, manufacturing, finance, and national security to build AI on the Palantir operating system.

### Key facts
- Northslope was founded by ex-Palantir engineers; its "forward-deployed engineer" (FDE) model mirrors the consulting-style implementation strategy that made Palantir sticky in government and large enterprise accounts
- This is the Deployment Company's second acquisition — following AI deployment firm Tomoro — since it launched in May 2026 with $4 billion in capital earmarked for M&A
- Northslope operates in mission-critical verticals: healthcare, energy, manufacturing, financial services, and national security
- Deal terms were not disclosed; the acquisition adds hundreds of FDEs to OpenAI's enterprise services bench

### Why it matters
AI model companies are no longer content to sell APIs — they're moving into the high-margin implementation and services market long dominated by Accenture and McKinsey. Northslope gives OpenAI the embedded-engineer playbook that made Palantir's government contracts so durable: once engineers are building your clients' workflows from the inside, switching costs soar. This deal signals that the next competitive battleground in enterprise AI is who owns the deployment relationship, not just whose model scores best on benchmarks.
