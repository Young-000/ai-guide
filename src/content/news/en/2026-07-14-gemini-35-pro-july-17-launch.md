**One-line summary**: Google DeepMind is reportedly targeting a July 17 launch for Gemini 3.5 Pro after a full architectural rebuild, with an unconfirmed 2-million-token context window and a new Deep Think reasoning layer as key claimed upgrades.

### Key facts
- Originally due in June, Gemini 3.5 Pro was pulled after engineers identified structural failures in recursive tool-calling pipelines and SVG generation — not cosmetic issues, requiring a full architectural rebuild
- Rumored improvements include a 2M-token context window, a Deep Think Reasoning Layer for complex multi-step tasks, and substantial gains in mathematical reasoning and SVG scene quality
- GPT-5.6 Sol and Grok 4.5 both launched publicly on July 9, increasing pressure on Google to deliver quickly
- Google has not officially confirmed the July 17 date, the 2M context window, pricing, or any benchmark scores — treat all circulating specs as unverified until an official blog post appears

### Why it matters
If Gemini 3.5 Pro ships on July 17 as reported, Google re-enters the frontier model race roughly five weeks after pulling its original build — a disciplined move if the rebuilt model is genuinely stronger. The willingness to delay rather than ship a flawed model reflects a shift from "ship first, patch later" toward quality-gating — exactly what enterprise customers stung by AI hallucination incidents have been demanding. A second delay, however, would compound the reputational damage and give OpenAI and Anthropic more time to consolidate enterprise accounts.
