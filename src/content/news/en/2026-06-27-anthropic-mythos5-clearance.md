**Summary**: The US Commerce Department cleared Anthropic's Claude Mythos 5 for selective release to over 100 vetted US institutions, lifting a two-week export block while Fable 5's status remains unresolved.

### Key Points
- Commerce Secretary Howard Lutnick sent a letter to Anthropic chief compute officer Tom Brown on June 26, citing "significant progress" in daily government-company negotiations since the June 12 shutdown
- Both Mythos 5 and Fable 5 were blocked after Amazon and others warned the models could be jailbroken for malicious purposes
- Only Mythos 5 is explicitly cleared in the letter — Fable 5 remains under restriction with no confirmed timeline
- Over 100 US companies and government agencies now have approved access; broader availability to follow in coming weeks

### Why It Matters
Mythos 5's partial restoration lets enterprise customers resume high-stakes workloads after a two-week freeze, but the asymmetric outcome — frontier capability restored for institutions, consumer-facing Fable 5 still blocked — reveals how US AI policy is increasingly managed through back-channel deal-making rather than transparent regulation.
