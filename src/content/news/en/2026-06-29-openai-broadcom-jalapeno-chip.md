**Summary**: OpenAI and Broadcom revealed 'Jalapeño,' OpenAI's first custom silicon chip purpose-built for LLM inference, marking a major step toward hardware independence from NVIDIA.

### Key Facts
- **Jalapeño** is an ASIC co-designed by OpenAI and Broadcom, unveiled June 24, 2026
- Optimized exclusively for LLM inference — targeting better power efficiency and throughput than general-purpose GPUs
- OpenAI used its own AI models to accelerate chip design, **shortening the development cycle**
- Joins Google (TPU), AWS (Trainium), and Microsoft (Maia) in the growing club of cloud providers with custom silicon

### Why It Matters
Compute cost is one of OpenAI's largest operating expenses. A proprietary inference chip gives OpenAI direct control over its hardware roadmap and a path to lowering per-token serving costs — a critical lever in the ongoing price war with Anthropic, Google, and open-source models. If Jalapeño delivers on efficiency promises, it could meaningfully shift margins at OpenAI's scale.
