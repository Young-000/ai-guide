**Summary**: Anthropic launched a public beta of Claude for Government on July 7, making Claude Code and Cowork available to U.S. federal agencies through a FedRAMP High authorized environment. An introductory $1/agency rate (unlimited seats) runs through August.

### Key Facts
- **Products included**: Claude Code for software modernization; Cowork for delegating memo writing, RFP reviews, casework, and presentations
- **Security posture**: FedRAMP High authorization with tamper-evident audit logs and ATO-support documentation
- **Governance controls**: Per-department spending allocation, configuration defaults, admin oversight — no separate cloud-provider contract required
- **Pricing**: $1 per agency (unlimited seats) through August 2026 — effectively a free trial for large agencies
- **Access**: Applications open at `claude.com/solutions/government`

### Why It Matters
Security certification and data sovereignty have long been the primary blockers for AI adoption in government. By coupling FedRAMP High authorization with an independent billing path (bypassing AWS or GCP relationships), Anthropic is lowering the procurement barrier significantly. The $1 pilot offer is designed to accelerate real-workload validation — a deliberate challenge to OpenAI and Google's established footholds in the federal AI market.
