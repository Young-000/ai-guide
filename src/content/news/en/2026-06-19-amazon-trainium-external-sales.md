**Summary**: Amazon is in active discussions to sell Trainium chips directly to third-party data centers, moving beyond its AWS-only rental model to challenge Nvidia on its home turf.

### Key Facts
- Bloomberg first reported that AWS is negotiating direct Trainium chip sales with multiple companies for their own data centers
- Amazon's custom chip business crossed a $20B annualized revenue run rate in Q1 2026, growing at triple-digit rates
- Amazon claims Trainium cuts AI inference costs by up to 80% versus comparable Nvidia H100 GPUs
- OpenAI and Anthropic have already committed to multi-gigawatt Trainium capacity through AWS agreements

### Why It Matters
Amazon has historically kept its custom silicon inside the AWS walled garden. Selling directly to other data centers would let Trainium compete head-to-head with Nvidia H100/B300 on price, potentially driving down infrastructure costs across the industry. It also signals Amazon's ambition to become a chip vendor, not just a cloud provider.
