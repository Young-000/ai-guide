**Summary**: Microsoft unveiled Microsoft Frontier Company on July 2, 2026—a new operating unit backed by $2.5 billion and roughly 6,000 engineers dedicated to deploying AI inside enterprise customers.

### Key Points
- Led by Rodrigo Kede Lima, former president of Microsoft Asia; announced by Commercial Business CEO Judson Althoff
- Uses a forward-deployed model: Microsoft engineers embed directly inside customer organizations to select technology, integrate enterprise data, redesign workflows, and ensure measurable ROI
- Early partners include the London Stock Exchange Group (LSEG), Unilever, Land O'Lakes, and Accenture
- Follows Amazon's $1 billion similar initiative announced two days earlier; OpenAI and Anthropic launched comparable ventures in May 2026

### Why It Matters
Three of the world's largest tech companies launching dedicated deployment subsidiaries within weeks of each other signals a structural shift: the AI competitive battlefield is moving beyond model benchmarks toward monetizing enterprise adoption at scale. The forward-deployed model—vendor engineers living inside customer teams—transforms AI from a software license into a managed outcome, raising switching costs and deepening customer lock-in for whoever executes best.
