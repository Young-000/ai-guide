**Summary**: OpenAI and Broadcom revealed the Jalapeño Intelligence Processor on June 24, an LLM-optimized inference accelerator that went from initial design to tape-out in nine months.

### Key Facts
- Nine-month design-to-tape-out cycle — both companies claim it's the fastest ever for high-performance ASICs
- Purpose-built for inference (running pre-trained models), not training; early tests show better performance per watt than current GPUs
- OpenAI used its own AI models to accelerate parts of the chip design and optimization process
- First deployment targeted for late 2026, part of a 10-gigawatt strategic collaboration with Broadcom extending through 2029

### Why It Matters
Inference cost is the dominant variable in AI service economics at scale. With Jalapeño, OpenAI is moving toward Apple-style vertical hardware integration — reducing Nvidia dependency and controlling the full stack from model to silicon. If performance claims hold in production, it could meaningfully shift the cost curves across the industry.
