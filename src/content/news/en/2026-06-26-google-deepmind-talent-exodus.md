**Summary**: A wave of high-profile departures from Google DeepMind — including a Nobel laureate — is fueling investor concern about Google's standing in the AI race.

### Key Facts
- **John Jumper** — co-developer of AlphaFold and 2024 Nobel Chemistry laureate — announced June 19 he is leaving DeepMind for Anthropic
- **Noam Shazeer** — widely credited as an architect of the Transformer model — departed for OpenAI
- **Jonas Adler and Alexander Pritzel** — key contributors to the Gemini model — confirmed moves to Anthropic on June 24
- Reports from Bloomberg, Fortune, and TechCrunch describe rattled investors questioning Google's ability to stay at the frontier

### Why It Matters
In AI research, people are the product. Losing four senior researchers in quick succession — especially one with a Nobel Prize — signals that rivals are successfully poaching the talent that drives model breakthroughs. Pre-IPO equity at Anthropic and OpenAI appears to be a compelling draw that Google's established compensation structure struggles to match.
