**Summary**: General Intuition, which trains AI world models on billions of gaming videos from Medal's 10-million-user platform, is raising $300M at a $2B valuation — its second major round in under a year.

### Key Facts
- New round: **$300M at ~$2B valuation**, in talks as of June 18, 2026
- Previous funding: **$134M seed** (8 months prior) from Khosla Ventures, General Catalyst
- New investors: **Jeff Bezos, Eric Schmidt**
- Data moat: Medal generates **2 billion gaming videos per year** from 10M monthly active users
- Founders: Pim de Witte (Medal co-founder) alongside world-modeling researchers Eloi Alonso, Adam Jelley, and Vincent Micheli

### Why It Matters
Most foundation models learn from language; General Intuition's models learn to act in space and time using first-person interactive video — the data type closest to how physical robots and autonomous agents must perceive the world. If the thesis holds, gaming data could become as strategically valuable as internet text was to early LLMs, with applications in robotics, autonomous vehicles, and enterprise agentic systems.
