**Summary**: The White House is poised to release voluntary AI model launch standards this week — a concrete follow-through on Trump's June 2 executive order — establishing 30-day pre-release federal review windows for frontier AI models with advanced capabilities.

### Key Points
- Follows Trump's June 2 EO "Promoting Advanced AI Innovation and Security," which created a "Secure Frontier Model Deployment" framework
- Core mechanism: models with advanced cyber capabilities reviewed by NSA and other federal agencies for up to **30 days** before broad commercial release
- GPT-5.6 Sol's general availability is reported to depend on satisfying these benchmarks
- Anthropic confirmed willingness to develop joint evaluation standards alongside Amazon, Microsoft, and Google
- Explicit limit: "nothing shall authorize mandatory licensing, preclearance, or permitting requirements" — strictly voluntary cooperation

### Why It Matters
This is the first time U.S. AI policy has moved from broad safety language to structured, timed checkpoints. Even on a voluntary basis, federal review windows and benchmark requirements risk hardening into de facto industry practice — reshaping launch timelines for future Claude and GPT releases and creating a policy template that other governments will study and adapt.
