**One-line summary**: Google has deployed Gemini 3.5 Flash as the global default for Search AI Mode and the Gemini app, replacing static results pages with dynamically generated, query-specific layouts for billions of users.

### Key facts
- Gemini 3.5 Flash is now the default for both the Gemini app and Google Search AI Mode globally — reaching billions of users
- Generative UI builds custom responses on the fly: interactive visualizations, tables, graphs, and simulations tailored to each query
- 3.5 Flash outperforms Gemini 3.1 Pro on agentic and coding benchmarks while running at Flash-tier speed and cost
- The rollout follows Google I/O 2026 announcements; full generative UI is available "free of charge this summer"
- Source links remain embedded within AI-generated responses rather than displayed as a traditional ranked list

### Why it matters
The upgrade marks a concrete inflection point in search's decade-long shift from keyword ranking to AI synthesis. By embedding source links inside a generative layout rather than listing them separately, Google is rewriting the implicit contract between publishers, advertisers, and search traffic. For users, richer and faster answers; for the broader web ecosystem, a structural reduction in referral clicks that accelerates the rethinking of SEO, content monetization, and how information surfaces online. The scale — billions of users on day one — makes this one of the largest simultaneous UX changes in internet history.
