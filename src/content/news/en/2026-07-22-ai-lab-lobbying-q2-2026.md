**Summary**: Newly filed federal disclosures show AI labs are now spending at Big Tech–level rates, with Anthropic taking the quarterly lead over major chipmakers.

### Key Facts
- **Anthropic**: $1.97M in Q2 (up 26% quarter-over-quarter) — a company record and above Nvidia's comparable spend
- **OpenAI**: $1.2M in Q2 (up 18%), also a quarterly record for the company
- Combined two-company Q2 spend: $3.17M — up 23% from Q1 2026
- Anthropic's H1 2026 total already exceeds its entire 2025 lobbying spend ($3.1M)
- Key focus areas: export controls, AI safety and cybersecurity standards; lobbyists met with Commerce, Treasury, the White House, and both chambers of Congress

### Why It Matters
Anthropic's spending spike directly follows the US export-control order that pulled Fable 5 offline for three weeks — a clear signal that the lab is treating Washington influence as a core business risk tool. As AI labs approach or exceed the lobbying budgets of established defense and semiconductor firms, their voice in upcoming AI legislation — on export rules, safety standards, and model access — will be harder to ignore.
