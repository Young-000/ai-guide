**Summary**: Anthropic expanded its Asia-Pacific footprint with a Seoul office and locked in a sweep of South Korean enterprise and government partnerships on June 17–18, 2026.

### Key Facts
- **Office**: Seoul is APAC hub #3 after Tokyo and Bengaluru; led by KiYoung Choi, former Snowflake Korea GM
- **Government MOU**: Partnership with South Korea's Ministry of Science and ICT covers AI safety evaluation, cyber offense/defense impact analysis, Korean-language misuse risk assessment, and red-team testing of autonomous AI agents
- **Enterprise rollouts**: NAVER (Claude Code across full engineering org), Samsung SDS (Claude Cowork + Claude Code across Samsung Electronics), LG CNS (LG Group-wide), Nexon (live-service game dev), Channel Corp (Channel Talk for 230,000+ businesses)
- **Research**: Claude access for up to 60 researchers in the National AI Research Lab consortium (KAIST, Korea University, Yonsei, POSTECH)

### Why It Matters
With Anthropic's IPO filing in progress and ongoing U.S. export control debates around Fable 5, the Seoul expansion signals a deliberate push to deepen ties with allied markets before any further restrictions take hold. The cluster of simultaneous enterprise adoptions — NAVER, Samsung, LG — also strengthens Anthropic's competitive position against OpenAI and Microsoft in B2B Asia-Pacific markets ahead of what will be a heavily watched public debut.
