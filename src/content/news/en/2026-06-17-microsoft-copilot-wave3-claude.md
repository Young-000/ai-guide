**Summary**: Microsoft's Wave 3 update adds Anthropic's Claude Fable 5 to Microsoft 365 Copilot, shifting the platform from a single-model assistant to a multi-model agent that routes each task to the best available model.

### Key facts
- **Wave 3 shift**: Copilot evolves from a conversational assistant into an autonomous agent capable of executing multi-step workflows across the M365 suite
- **Claude Fable 5 integration**: Available June 2026 in M365 Copilot — GPT and Claude run side-by-side, with Copilot auto-selecting the model best suited for each task
- **Copilot Cowork**: A Claude-powered long-running task agent for complex, hours-long workflows; priced at $30/user/month as an add-on
- **Agent 365 GA**: Launched May 1 at $15/user/month — a governance platform for monitoring and managing custom AI agents across the organization
- **Frontier Suite (E7)**: $99/user/month bundle combining Agent 365, Entra, Defender, Intune, and Purview

### Why it matters
Microsoft absorbing Anthropic's model into its core productivity suite without breaking its OpenAI partnership shows that multi-vendor AI stacks are the new norm in enterprise. For Anthropic, the deal provides a massive distribution channel into corporate accounts it would struggle to reach directly. For enterprise IT, the move raises new questions around governance, data residency, and which vendor's safety policies apply when Claude and GPT are running side-by-side on the same document.
