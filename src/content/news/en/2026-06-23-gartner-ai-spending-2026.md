**Summary**: Gartner's May 2026 forecast places total global AI spending at $2.52 trillion this year — a 47% jump — with AI agent software outpacing every other category at +139%, from $86.4B in 2025 to $206.5B in 2026, and projected to nearly double again to $376.3B by 2027.

### Key Facts
- **Total AI spend**: $2.52T in 2026, up from ~$1.7T in 2025 (+47%); hardware, data-center buildout, and cloud remain the largest absolute categories
- **AI agent software**: $86.4B → $206.5B in 2026 (+139%); forecast to reach $376.3B in 2027 — making it the fastest-growing enterprise software segment tracked by Gartner
- **Demand drivers**: Enterprise adoption of multi-step agentic pipelines for automation and decision support, led by Microsoft Copilot, Salesforce Agentforce, and purpose-built vertical AI vendors
- **Gartner warning**: A separate May 2026 report notes many enterprise AI investments are increasing costs without delivering measurable returns in the near term — spend growth does not equal ROI

### Why It Matters
The 139% surge in agent software represents actual procurement decisions, not aspirational budgets. For developers, it means the market has already priced in a shift from prompt-response inference to orchestrated, multi-turn agent workflows. Companies choosing platforms, frameworks, and hiring strategies today are positioning for a segment Gartner expects to be worth nearly $400 billion by next year.
