**Summary**: Google will pay SpaceX $920 million a month for access to roughly 110,000 NVIDIA GPUs at its Colossus data center in Tennessee, underscoring how even the world's largest cloud provider can't build fast enough to keep up with AI demand.

### Key Facts
- Contract runs October 2026 – June 2029 at $920M/month; reduced ramp-up fee applies through September 2026
- ~110,000 NVIDIA GPUs plus CPUs and memory included; either party can exit with 90 days' notice after December 2026
- Google may terminate immediately if SpaceX misses GPU capacity targets by end of September 2026
- Anthropic separately agreed to pay $1.25B/month for the same Colossus facility, signed in late May 2026

### Why It Matters
The hyperscalers' own data-center build-out can't keep pace with AI compute demand, so they're effectively writing SpaceX a blank check to bridge the gap. For SpaceX, projected 2026 data center revenue is on track to exceed its combined Starlink, launch, and AI proceeds from 2025 — a dramatic business-model expansion for a company that started with rockets.
