**Summary**: ByteDance announced Seedance 2.5 on June 23, positioning it as the first AI video model to generate a native 30-second 4K clip in one continuous pass — no clip-stitching workaround required.

### Key facts
- Accepts up to 50 simultaneous reference inputs (images, video clips, audio) in a single generation call
- Localized editing lets users repaint a specific region of a frame while leaving the rest untouched
- Currently in enterprise beta; public launch targeted for early July 2026
- Predecessor Seedance 2.0 topped independent blind-preference rankings ahead of Google and leading Chinese competitors — but ByteDance paused its global rollout after every major Hollywood studio sent cease-and-desist letters over alleged copyright infringement

### Why it matters
Stitching together multiple short clips has been the central workaround — and the central quality bottleneck — in AI video production. Native 30-second 4K output removes that constraint and pushes Seedance 2.5 into territory directly usable for advertising, short-form content, and film pre-visualization. The unresolved copyright liability from the previous generation, however, could slow Western enterprise adoption and may invite renewed legal pressure if Seedance 2.5 is trained on similar data.
