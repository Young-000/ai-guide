**Summary**: Crunchbase reports global startup investment hit $510B in H1 2026 — already above 2025's full-year $440B — with OpenAI and Anthropic together pulling in $217B, or 43% of all global venture capital.

### Key Points
- **$510B** in H1 2026 surpasses all of 2025 ($440B) in just six months
- OpenAI + Anthropic raised $217B combined; Anthropic alone closed a $65B round in Q2
- More than **70% of Q2 VC** went to AI companies, up from under 50% one year prior
- 16 rounds of $1B+ closed in Q2, totaling $108.6B — 53% of the quarter's entire investment
- Mega-rounds are expanding beyond foundation models into AI infrastructure, defense, robotics, and healthcare

### Why It Matters
The numbers confirm venture capital has effectively restructured around AI. Concentration at this scale — two companies absorbing 43% of all global VC — is historically unprecedented. It signals both the scale of capital chasing frontier AI and how little is reaching other sectors, a dynamic limited partners are increasingly flagging as they reassess portfolio diversification.
