**One-line summary**: TSMC's Q2 2026 revenue hit approximately $39.62 billion — a new all-time quarterly record — fueled by AI accelerator demand from Nvidia, Apple, and other key customers.

### Key facts
- Q2 revenue: ~$39.62 billion (NT$1.27 trillion), beating consensus estimates by roughly 0.5%
- 36% year-over-year growth, the highest rate in several quarters
- Advanced nodes (7nm and below) accounted for the bulk of wafer revenue and continued to push gross margins higher
- TSMC is the primary manufacturer of chips for Nvidia's data-center GPU lineup and Apple's A- and M-series silicon

### Why it matters
TSMC's quarterly results function as the most reliable thermometer for the AI infrastructure cycle. A 36% growth rate — despite macro headwinds and ongoing questions about GPU oversupply — confirms that enterprise demand for frontier AI compute has not moderated through mid-2026. The beat also strengthens the bull case for continued hyperscaler capex in the second half of the year and validates the thesis that the AI hardware build-out is a multi-year, sustained investment cycle rather than a one-time burst.
