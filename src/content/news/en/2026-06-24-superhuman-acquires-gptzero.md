**One-liner**: Superhuman, the AI-powered email client, has bought GPTZero — the startup that detects AI-generated content — putting creation and detection under one roof.

### Key Facts
- GPTZero was founded by Princeton grad Edward Tian as a senior thesis project; grew to **19 million registered users** and **$30M ARR** with a valuation exceeding $88M
- Capabilities acquired: AI text detection, plagiarism analysis, hallucination detection, authorship verification, and AI-generated image recognition
- GPTZero's technology will integrate into Superhuman's **Go AI assistant**; acquisition terms not disclosed
- The irony: Superhuman helps users write with AI while GPTZero's core product detects AI-written content

### Why It Matters
Bundling AI detection into an email platform makes authenticity verification invisible and automatic — a meaningful upgrade for enterprise customers worried about impersonation, AI-assisted phishing, or compliance with AI disclosure mandates. The acquisition signals that "AI authenticity" is graduating from an academic niche to a core enterprise requirement that productivity platforms must address natively.
