**Summary**: Meta's next AI model, codenamed Watermelon, is matching GPT-5.5 on internal benchmarks — though the specific evaluations have not been published.

### Key Facts
- Announced by Alexandr Wang, Meta's Superintelligence Chief, at an internal town hall on July 3
- Watermelon uses roughly 10x the compute of its predecessor Avocado (publicly released as Muse Spark in April)
- Model is still in training; no public benchmark table or third-party evaluation released yet
- Wang hinted publicly on X that a major Muse Spark update with coding and agentic improvements is coming soon

### Why It Matters
Meta signaling frontier-level performance from an internally trained model — while keeping the benchmarks private — is a directional indicator that the open-source LLM race is tightening again. Until independent evaluations land, treat it as a strong strategic intent signal, not a confirmed capability ranking.
