**Summary**: Google's next flagship LLM was promised on stage at I/O with a one-month deadline. That deadline is June 30. As of June 19, the model has not shipped publicly.

### Key Facts
- **The promise**: At Google I/O on May 19, Sundar Pichai told the live audience "give us until next month" — prompting audible groans
- **Announced specs**: 2M-token context window, "Deep Think" reasoning mode, frontier multimodal understanding
- **Current status**: Limited Vertex AI enterprise preview only; not available in Gemini app, AI Studio, or consumer subscriptions
- **Prediction market odds**: Polymarket gives approximately 50–55% probability of a public release before June 30

### Why It Matters
Gemini 3.5 Pro is Google's answer to GPT-5.5 and Claude Fable 5 in the multimodal and long-context race. With 11 days left, developers face a practical choice: keep building on Flash, or wait for Pro. If Google misses its own deadline, it will be a visible credibility setback in a market where shipping velocity increasingly signals competitive strength.
