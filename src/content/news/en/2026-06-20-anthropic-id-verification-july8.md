**Summary**: Anthropic announced that starting July 8, 2026, it will require identity verification — photo ID and live selfie via Persona — for some Claude Free, Pro, and Max account holders flagged by its abuse-detection systems.

### Key Facts
- **Effective date**: July 8, 2026; mandated through an updated privacy policy
- **Scope**: Consumer tiers (Free, Pro, Max) only; enterprise accounts exempt
- **Verification flow**: Age verification via Yoti; identity verification via Persona — may collect government ID photos, video recordings, user images, and facial geometry templates
- Anthropic states verification is triggered only when its systems detect activity suggesting abuse or policy violations — not applied universally to all users

### Why It Matters
This is one of the first major moves by a frontier AI lab to tie biometric identity to model access, coming days after the Fable 5 export ban linked to SK Telecom's access rights. The policy puts Anthropic in territory previously occupied by regulated financial services — collecting biometric data to satisfy due-diligence and compliance requirements. Whether OpenAI and Google follow with similar requirements will establish industry norms for AI access control.
