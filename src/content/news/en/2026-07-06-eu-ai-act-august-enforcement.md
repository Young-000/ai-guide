**Summary**: On August 2, 2026, the EU AI Act's penalty enforcement powers over GPAI providers activate, along with Article 50 transparency obligations — making this the most consequential AI compliance deadline since the regulation's adoption.

### Key Facts
- August 2: GPAI providers face fines up to €35M or 7% of global annual turnover; national market surveillance authorities gain full investigative and sanctioning powers
- Transparency obligations (Article 50) take effect for most AI systems placing content in front of EU users
- High-risk AI system enforcement extended 16 months — harmonized standards from CEN-CENELEC won't be ready until at least Q4 2026
- The Commission retains power to demand model access and order system recalls for GPAI models

### Why It Matters
Even with the high-risk extension, GPAI obligations — transparency reporting, copyright summaries, technical documentation — are live on August 2. Every major LLM provider serving the EU market (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google, Meta) enters a compliance phase that now carries real financial consequences, not just advisory pressure. Companies that haven't filed their required documentation are already behind.
