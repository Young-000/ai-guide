**Summary**: Anthropic's zero-day exploit-hunting model launched publicly as Claude Fable 5 on June 9 — then a U.S. government export-control order forced Anthropic to cut off global access just three days later.

### Key Facts
- **April 2026**: Claude Mythos Preview launched via Project Glasswing (restricted to AWS, Microsoft, Apple, and CrowdStrike)
- Core capability: **autonomous discovery and chaining of zero-day exploits** across major operating systems and browsers
- **June 9**: Public release as **Claude Fable 5** — high-risk biology and cybersecurity responses blocked, routed to Claude Opus 4.8
- **June 12**: U.S. government export-control directive → access to Claude Fable 5 and Claude Mythos 5 suspended globally

### Why It Matters
A frontier AI model being suspended by government order three days after launch is unprecedented. It signals that powerful cybersecurity AI is now treated as a controlled strategic asset on par with weapons technology, setting a precedent that will shape how regulators approach all future dual-use AI systems.
