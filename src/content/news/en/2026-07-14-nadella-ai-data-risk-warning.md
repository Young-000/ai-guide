**Summary**: Microsoft's CEO went public with a pointed warning that enterprises using frontier AI models are inadvertently handing their competitive edge to the companies they're paying.

### Key Facts
- Nadella coined the **"reverse information paradox"**: companies pay for AI with cash, then pay again with "something even more valuable" — the proprietary domain knowledge they must inject for the model to be useful
- Models distill institutional know-how from prompts, agentic tool calls, and especially user corrections: "every correction is distilled into institutional knowledge"
- Proposed mitigations: retain data ownership, build proprietary learning environments, and implement **multi-model orchestration layers** to avoid vendor lock-in
- Nadella pitched Microsoft Azure AI Foundry as the solution — a pointed competitive move against OpenAI and Anthropic

### Why It Matters
As AI agents gain deeper access to internal enterprise data, the risk Nadella describes grows sharply. Companies building critical workflows on a single proprietary model may be funding their future competitors. The warning also signals Microsoft repositioning itself as a neutral AI platform — against its own portfolio companies.
