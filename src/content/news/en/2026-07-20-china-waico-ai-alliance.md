**Summary**: China formalized a rival AI governance order on July 16, founding a 29-nation intergovernmental organization that explicitly sidesteps Western regulatory frameworks.

### Key Facts
- WAICO signed into existence by 29 countries on July 16, on the eve of the World AI Conference (WAIC 2026) in Shanghai
- Founding members skew Global South: Indonesia, Brazil, Malaysia, South Africa, Senegal, Russia, Pakistan among them
- UN Secretary-General António Guterres attended the signing ceremony
- Member countries can adopt AI rules that need not align with the EU AI Act, OECD AI Principles, or the G7 Hiroshima Process
- WAIC 2026 debuted over 300 AI products and services alongside the launch

### Why It Matters
WAICO formalizes a bifurcation that has been building for years: one AI governance track anchored by the EU and G7, another led by China and the Global South. If a large share of the world's population and markets operate under WAICO-compatible rules — which may omit the transparency mandates and high-risk approval gates of Western regimes — multinational AI companies will face genuine dual-compliance risk. It also undercuts any ambition for a single global AI treaty, replacing it with competing governance blocs.
