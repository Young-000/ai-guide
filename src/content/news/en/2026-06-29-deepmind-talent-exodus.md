**Summary**: In a rapid succession of departures, six of Google DeepMind's top researchers left for rivals, triggering a market selloff that erased roughly $270 billion from Alphabet's valuation.

### Key Facts
- Six exits in 10 days (June 18–28): Noam Shazeer (Transformer co-author, Gemini co-lead) → OpenAI; John Jumper (Nobel laureate, AlphaFold lead) → Anthropic; Jonas Adler (Gemini coding lead) → Anthropic; Alexander Pritzel (pretraining specialist) → Anthropic; Arthur Conmy (Gemini 2.5 contributor) → Anthropic; a sixth researcher to Meta
- Alphabet stock fell ~7% on June 22 alone — its steepest single-day drop in over a year — with further declines as later exits were reported
- Driving factors include Anthropic's pre-IPO equity (company valued at ~$965B), internal compute resource conflicts at Google, and Anthropic's expanding safety and scientific AI agenda

### Why It Matters
DeepMind invented or co-developed the transformer architecture, AlphaFold, and much of the infrastructure underlying modern AI. Losing the people behind those breakthroughs — in rapid, public succession — raises structural questions about whether Gemini can keep pace at the frontier. It also signals that Anthropic's equity story, not just its research agenda, is now a powerful recruiting tool.
