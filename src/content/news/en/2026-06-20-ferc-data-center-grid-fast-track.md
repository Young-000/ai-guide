**One-line summary**: FERC has declared AI data center grid access a national priority, ordering the six largest U.S. grid operators to produce fast-lane interconnection plans within 60 days.

### Key Points
- FERC voted unanimously on June 18 to direct PJM, MISO, SPP, CAISO, ISO-NE, and NYISO to overhaul their interconnection processes for large electricity users
- Operators have 60 days to justify or redesign how they handle data center connection requests; a separate 30-day deadline requires reports on existing customer power adequacy
- Data centers bear the costs of their own interconnection — FERC's directive targets process speed, not electricity generation sourcing
- The order fulfills a 2025 request from Energy Secretary Chris Wright to accelerate grid access for large-load facilities, framing power as AI national infrastructure

### Why It Matters
Grid connection delays have been cited as the single biggest bottleneck for U.S. AI infrastructure expansion. With this order, the federal government is explicitly linking energy policy to AI competitiveness. If grid operators comply, it could meaningfully accelerate data center build-outs — which translates directly into faster availability of training and inference capacity for AI developers.
