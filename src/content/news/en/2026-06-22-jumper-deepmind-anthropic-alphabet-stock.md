**Summary**: AlphaFold's lead researcher and Nobel laureate John Jumper is departing Google DeepMind for Anthropic, triggering a 7% drop in Alphabet shares and wiping out roughly $250 billion in market cap.

### Key Facts
- Jumper spent **9 years** at DeepMind as VP and Engineering Fellow, leading **AlphaFold** development and sharing the **2024 Nobel Prize in Chemistry**
- He will join **Anthropic**; specific role not yet disclosed
- The announcement follows Gemini co-lead **Noam Shazeer's** move to OpenAI just days earlier
- Alphabet shares fell as much as **7.2%** intraday on June 22 — the stock's worst day in a year — with combined losses across both departures estimated at **~$250 billion** in market cap

### Why It Matters
Google DeepMind has long been the gold standard for foundational AI research, but back-to-back defections to IPO-bound rivals signal a talent dynamic that markets are now pricing in. As Anthropic and OpenAI ramp toward public offerings, their equity upside is pulling away researchers that salary alone may not retain — making talent concentration a first-order competitive risk for incumbents.
