**In brief**: NAVER will deploy a full-stack NVIDIA DSX AI factory at its GAK Sejong hyperscale data center — 55 MW by H1 2027, scaling to 100 MW, 200 MW, and eventually 1 GW — to build sovereign AI infrastructure for Korean government, enterprise, and cloud customers.

### Key facts
- Deployment roadmap: 55 MW (H1 2027) → 100 MW (H2 2027) → 200 MW (2028) → 1 GW long-term
- Built on NVIDIA DSX, providing high-density accelerated compute with energy efficiency and disaster-resilience
- NAVER becomes the first Korean company in NVIDIA's Nemotron Coalition, contributing to open model pre-training, post-training, and RL
- Compute will power next-generation HyperCLOVA X, the Seoul World Model, and NAVER's agentic AI services
- Jensen Huang's Korea visit also produced AI factory agreements with SK hynix, SK Telecom, LG Group, and Hyundai Motor Group

### Why it matters
South Korea is building one of the world's first nationally-scaled sovereign AI compute bases outside the US, EU, and China. For NAVER, this transforms GAK Sejong from a cloud provider into an AI factory capable of training frontier-class models domestically — reducing dependence on foreign compute and positioning HyperCLOVA X as a credible global multilingual model challenger.
