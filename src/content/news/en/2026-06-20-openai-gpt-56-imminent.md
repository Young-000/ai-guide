**One-line summary**: GPT-5.6 is expected within days — Polymarket gives June 22–28 an 83% probability, and OpenAI's chief scientist has reportedly called it a meaningful improvement over GPT-5.5.

### Key Points
- Polymarket bettors placed $960,000+ on a June 22–28 window, assigning 83% probability — the highest concentration of any launch window
- OpenAI's chief scientist reportedly confirmed GPT-5.6 represents a notable improvement in reasoning depth and agentic workflow handling compared to GPT-5.5
- An internal release candidate codenamed "kindle-alpha" briefly appeared on the Design Arena testing platform before being removed (unconfirmed)
- No official announcement or API model string as of June 20; improved token efficiency and lower operational costs are cited as headline features

### Why It Matters
GPT-5.5 launched in April. A successor arriving in under two months signals an aggressive release cadence that outpaces Anthropic and Google's public schedules. For API-dependent developers and enterprises, the pace of OpenAI model changes means migration planning is becoming a recurring engineering task rather than a once-a-year event.
