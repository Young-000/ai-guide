**One-liner**: Trump's NSPM-11 directs the entire U.S. national security apparatus to accelerate AI adoption, replacing Biden-era restraint with a four-pillar framework built around deployment speed and commercial model access.

### Key Facts
- Signed June 5, 2026; supersedes Biden's National Security Memorandum-25 (NSM-25, 2024)
- Four pillars: Adoption (accelerate deployment), Adaptation (leverage commercial and open-source AI), Assurance (prevent unauthorized disabling or modification of deployed AI), Accountability (ban AI-enabled censorship, ideological bias, or unlawful surveillance)
- Permits military and intelligence agencies to use frontier commercial and open-source models rather than requiring government-only builds
- Requires AI systems in national security contexts to be protected from external interference or unauthorized shutdown

### Why It Matters
This is a philosophical pivot: Biden's framework prioritized risk controls before deployment; NSPM-11 prioritizes deployment velocity. For AI labs, it opens direct pathways to defense and intelligence contracts at frontier-capability levels — while the explicit bans on AI-enabled bias and surveillance add political guardrails that are likely to shape procurement requirements across the industry.
